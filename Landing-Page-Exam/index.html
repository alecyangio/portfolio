What is cardiovascular care and why is it important?

Cardiovascular care is provided by different types of doctors, such as a cardiologist or interventional cardiologist and can diagnose and treat conditions affecting the heart, arteries and blood vessels. Cardiovascular care plays a vital role in maintaining overall health and reducing the risk of serious illnesses such as heart attacks, strokes and high blood pressure.

Cardiovascular disease treatment matters because it:

  • Saves lives. Heart disease is the leading cause of death in the U.S., making cardiovascular care essential for reducing mortality and improving life expectancy.

  • Focuses on prevention. Many heart-related conditions can be prevented by managing risk factors like high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, poor diet, lack of exercise, and smoking.

  • Helps manage chronic conditions. Ongoing care is crucial for people with chronic heart conditions to prevent complications and maintain a better quality of life.

  • May improve long-term health outcomes. Timely and effective cardiovascular care can lead to fewer hospital visits, better recovery rates and healthier lives.
